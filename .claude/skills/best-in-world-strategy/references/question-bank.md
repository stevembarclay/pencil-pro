# Red-Team Question Bank

Use this bank when strategy needs deeper pressure testing.
Select the smallest useful subset instead of asking every question.

## 1) Problem Framing

- What exact decision are we making, and by when?
- What problem are we actually solving?
- If we did nothing for 90 days, what happens?
- What would make this decision irrelevant in six months?

## 2) Assumptions

- Which assumption, if false, breaks the plan?
- Which assumptions are conventions instead of causal truths?
- What are we treating as fixed that is actually negotiable?
- Where are we confusing correlation with causation?

## 3) Best-in-World Baseline

- What would a world-class expert insist is non-negotiable?
- Which recommendation is high impact but uncomfortable?
- Which recommendation is expensive but critical?
- Which recommendation sounds elite but has weak causal value?

## 4) Constraints and Tradeoffs

- Which hard constraints are real versus inherited?
- Which soft constraint is silently driving a weak compromise?
- Are we optimizing for certainty, speed, upside, or optics?
- What are we unwilling to pay, and what risk does that buy?

## 5) Risk and Failure Modes

- How does this fail fast? How does it fail slowly?
- What second-order effect appears after initial success?
- What external dependency can fail outside our control?
- What single point of failure are we accepting?

## 6) Adversarial Lens

- If a hostile expert wanted to discredit this plan, where is the first attack?
- What would a skeptical board member call naive?
- What would security, legal, or compliance challenge immediately?
- Which claim has the weakest logic?

## 7) Execution Reality

- What must be true operationally in week 1?
- What capability gap will block execution?
- Which part requires unrealistic coordination?
- What will teams stop doing to create capacity?

## 8) Decision Hygiene

- Is this a one-way door or two-way door?
- What minimum reversible step gives meaningful learning?
- What triggers rollback?
- What evidence would force strategy change?

## 9) Economics and Opportunity Cost

- What is max acceptable loss in time, money, and reputation?
- What are we not doing if we choose this?
- What is expected value of waiting 30 days?
- What does cheap now cost later in rework or risk?

## 10) Learning Loop

- What leading indicators say we are wrong?
- What counts as signal versus vanity progress?
- What decision rule do we use at checkpoint one?
- What should be updated in this strategy after next cycle?
